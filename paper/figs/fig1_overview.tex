\caption{Two-stage architecture for weakly supervised temporal uncertainty
localization. \textbf{Stage~1} (top) trains an ACT-ViT sequence classifier
on the full activation tensor $A \in \mathbb{R}^{L \times T \times D}$: a
per-LLM linear adapter projects the hidden dimension into a shared space,
followed by max pooling, ViT patching over the layer--token plane, the
ACT-ViT backbone, and a linear head producing a sequence-level uncertainty
score. \textbf{Stage~2} (bottom) freezes the ACT-ViT encoder and applies
it to centered token windows, yielding per-window embeddings in
$\mathbb{R}^{T \times d_{\text{model}}}$. A residual Mamba-2 + LayerNorm
temporal head emits per-window logits, which a top-$k$ multiple-instance
learning rule aggregates into a sequence-level score. The per-window
logits also localize the generated-token regions whose hidden-state
activations most contribute to the prediction (highlighted in the
example response).}
