\caption{Three-stage Temp-ViT architecture for weakly supervised
temporal uncertainty localization. \textbf{Stage~1} (top) trains an
ACT-ViT sequence classifier on per-atom activation tensors
$A \in \mathbb{R}^{L \times T \times D}$: a per-LLM linear adapter
projects the hidden dimension into a shared space, followed by
adaptive max pooling, ViT patching over the layer--token plane, the
ACT-ViT backbone, and a linear head producing an atom-level
uncertainty score. \textbf{Stage~2} (middle) freezes the encoder and
applies it to centered length-$W$ token windows, producing per-window
embeddings in $\mathbb{R}^{T \times d_{\text{model}}}$ consumed by a
residual Mamba-2 + LayerNorm temporal head. A top-$k$
multiple-instance learning rule aggregates the per-window logits into
an atom-level score supervised against the same atom label as
Stage~1. \textbf{Stage~3} (bottom) keeps the encoder frozen,
initializes the Mamba-2 head from Stage~2, and fine-tunes it on whole
passages: the per-token logits are smoothed along the token axis with
a soft-top-$k$ window and aggregated within each atom span
$(\ell_m, r_m)$ into a per-atom logit, supervised by a per-atom
binary cross-entropy against the noisy auxiliary labels
$\tilde{y}_m$ obtained from atomic-fact verification. Stage~3 trains
the head to discriminate \emph{across} atoms within a passage rather
than scoring each atom in isolation, sharpening the per-window scores
it produces at inference (highlighted in the example response).}
\label{fig:overview}
\end{figure*}
