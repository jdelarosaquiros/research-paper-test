\caption{Three-stage Temp-ViT.
\textbf{Stage~1} (top): an ACT-ViT encoder maps a per-claim activation
tensor $A \in \mathbb{R}^{L \times T \times D}$ to a claim-level
uncertainty score via Pool $\to$ per-LLM linear adapter $\to$ ViT
$\to$ linear head.
\textbf{Stage~2} (middle): a Mamba-2 temporal head is trained on
centered length-$W$ token windows while the encoder is fine-tuned at
$0.1\times$ learning rate; per-window logits are aggregated by
top-$k$ MIL into a claim-level score.
\textbf{Stage~3} (bottom): the encoder is frozen and the head is
refined on whole passages with noisy per-claim labels $\tilde{y}_m$
from atomic-fact verification; per-token logits are smoothed by a
soft-topk window (temperature $\tau_w$) and aggregated within each
claim span $(\ell_m, r_m)$ by a soft-max-in-spans (temperature
$\tau_\text{seg}$) to yield per-claim probabilities $p_m$, trained
with $\sum_m \mathrm{BCE}(p_m, \tilde{y}_m)$. Both temperatures
anneal $1.0 \to 0.1$ over training.}
\label{fig:overview}
\end{figure*}
