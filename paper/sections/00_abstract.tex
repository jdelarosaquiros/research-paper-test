\begin{abstract}
Long-form LLM outputs often mix supported and unsupported claims, requiring detectors that localize unreliable spans rather than assign only a response-level label.
Internal representations contain useful hallucination signals, but current mechanistic detectors read them from the wrong unit: fixed token positions are too narrow, pooled full-response activation maps are too global, and isolated claim classifiers miss boundary evidence.
We introduce Temp-ViT, a weakly supervised temporal localization model that encodes local layer-token activation windows and propagates window-level uncertainty evidence across the generated text.
Temp-ViT is trained with a three-stage framework: claim-level encoder pretraining, top-$k$ MIL window localization, and cross-claim refinement over mixed-content windows.
Across RAGTruth, FactScore, LongFact, and LM-Polygraph, Temp-ViT improves token-level AUROC by up to $30\%$ over pooled-activation and confidence-based baselines, remains competitive on short-form uncertainty tasks, and runs $25\times$ faster on long passages.
These results identify local activation windows as the inference unit for scalable token, sentence, and claim-level hallucination localization.
\end{abstract}
