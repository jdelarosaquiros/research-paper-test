\begin{abstract}
Hallucinations in Large Language Models (LLMs) pose significant reliability concerns, especially in long-form generations where supported and unsupported claims can appear within the same response. Recent work shows that LLM hidden-states activations contain useful signals for hallucination detection. However, existing mechanistic detectors either rely on predetermined layer or token positions, pooling activations across the full-response into a global score, or classifying extracted claims in isolation, missing signals from tokens before the claim. We introduce Temporal Perception Encoder (TPE), a weakly supervised temporal localization framework that predicts hallucination risk from token-centered windows of hidden-state activations. TPE encodes each local layer-token activation window with a Perception Encoder and propagates hallucination signals across the response with a temporal head. Using top-k multiple instance learning, TPE learns token-level localization from claim or sequence labels without requiring supervision at the window or token level. Experiments on long-form biographical generation, open-ended factual QA, retrieval-augmented QA, and other tasks show that TPE improves hallucination detection and localization over state-of-the-art confidence-based and mechanistic baselines.
\end{abstract}
