\begin{abstract}
Long-form LLM outputs often mix supported and unsupported claims, requiring detectors that localize unreliable spans rather than assigning only a response-level label.
Internal representations contain useful hallucination signals, but current mechanistic detectors read them from the wrong unit: fixed token positions are too narrow, pooled full-response activation maps are too global, and isolated claim classifiers miss boundary evidence.
We introduce Temp-ViT, a weakly supervised temporal localization model that encodes local layer-token activation windows and propagates window-level uncertainty evidence across the generated text.
We achieve this localization by formulating hallucination detection as weakly supervised temporal localization over token-centered activation windows, where claim- and sequence-level labels supervise top-$k$ window selection and temporal scoring without token-level annotations.
Experiments on RAGTruth, FactScore, LongFact, and LM-Polygraph show that Temp-ViT improves token-level AUROC by up to $30\%$ over pooled-activation and confidence-based baselines while running $25\times$ faster on long passages.
\end{abstract}
