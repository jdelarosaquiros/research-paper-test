\begin{abstract}
Detecting hallucinations in long-form LLM outputs requires methods that are both fine-grained and efficient.
Existing uncertainty methods either struggle with semantic similarity, require expensive consistency sampling, or lose localized signals by probing isolated layer-token positions or pooling over long sequences.
We introduce a temporal uncertainty localization model that predicts token-level hallucination uncertainty from hidden-state activations.
Our model analyzes activation patterns across layers within local token windows and propagates window-level uncertainty evidence across the generated text.
We train it with a three-stage framework that progresses from claim-level hallucination detection to localized token-window supervision.
Across long-form hallucination benchmarks (RAGTruth, FActScore, LongFact) and the LM-Polygraph uncertainty benchmark, our method improves token-level AUROC by up to $30\%$ over pooled activation-tensor and confidence-based baselines, remains competitive on short-form tasks, and runs $25\times$ faster on long passages.
These results show that windowed hidden-state localization enables scalable token-level hallucination detection for long-form generation.
\end{abstract}
