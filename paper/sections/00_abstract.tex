\begin{abstract}
Detecting hallucinations in Large Language Model-generated text is crucial for
their safe deployment. Recent mechanistic approaches show promise by using
internal hidden states as uncertainty signals, but they face two important
limitations. Some methods probe only isolated layer--token positions, missing
uncertainty signals distributed across layers and generated tokens. Others
process richer activation tensors but are not equipped for long-form
generation -- pooling over the token axis collapses the signal as responses
grow longer -- or are too inefficient to run as tokens are produced. In this
paper, we introduce a temporal uncertainty localization framework that
detects hallucinations per token by finding uncertainty signals in activation
patterns from across all layers of its own and neighboring tokens. We train it
in three stages: \textbf{Stage 1} pre-trains an encoder on per-claim
segments to learn uncertainty patterns; \textbf{Stage 2} freezes it and
trains a temporal head over per-token windows with top-$k$ MIL to
localize signals within a claim; \textbf{Stage 3} refines the head on
whole passages with per-claim labels so it ranks windows across claims. On long-form hallucination corpora (RAGTruth, FactScore,
LongFact) and the LM-Polygraph short-form benchmark across three
instruction-tuned LLMs, our method improves sentence-level AUROC by
$8$--$25$ points over the strongest pooled activation-tensor baseline
while running $\approx 25\times$ faster on long passages.
\end{abstract}
