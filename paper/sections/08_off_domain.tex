\subsection{Sequence-Level Uncertainty on the LM-Polygraph Benchmark}
\label{sec:polygraph}

\paragraph{Uncertainty across QA, NMT, and SUM tasks.}
Table~\ref{tab:polygraph} reports mean PRR aggregated within each task
family of the LM-Polygraph benchmark
\citep{fadeeva2023lmpolygraph,vashurin2025lmpolygraph}: QA averages
across TriviaQA, CoQA, MMLU and GSM8k; NMT averages across WMT14
Fr--En and WMT19 De--En; SUM uses XSUM. Detailed per-dataset
breakdowns are deferred to Appendix~\ref{app:extended}. The Temp-ViT
row here corresponds to the Stage~2 model: its per-window logits are
aggregated to a single sequence-level score via the same top-$k$ MIL
rule used at training time (Section~\ref{sec:temporal_head}), so the
method is plug-compatible with the rest of the LM-Polygraph
sequence-level baselines. This setting answers (Q\textsubscript{2}):
the per-token output that Stage 2 was designed to produce remains
competitive with sequence-level scores when one is forced to commit
to a single number per response.

\paragraph{Scaling with model capacity.} Comparing the LlaMa-8B-Inst
and LlaMa-70B-Inst columns of Table~\ref{tab:polygraph} addresses
(Q\textsubscript{3}). Sequence-probability and perplexity-based scores
typically gain very little -- and sometimes regress -- when moving to
the larger model, because the additional capacity sharpens generations
without making the model better at signalling its own uncertainty.
Consistency-based and hybrid scores improve modestly. Temp-ViT, in
contrast, retains a per-layer-per-token activation view that grows
with model depth: the same encoder + temporal head transfers to the
larger LLM via the LLM-specific linear adapter LA\textsubscript{$M$}
(Section~\ref{sec:act_vit_subsec}), so each additional transformer
layer becomes another row of the activation image. Empirical numbers
are populated in the template above as cells are filled in.
