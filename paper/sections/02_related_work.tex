\section{Related Work}

\subsection{Uncertainty estimation for LLMs}

Confidence-based methods derive uncertainty from token-level
probabilities of a single generated sequence: sequence probability,
predictive entropy, mean token entropy
\citep{malinin2021uncertainty}, and SAR \citep{duan2024shifting},
which reweights tokens by their relevance.
\citet{schweighofer2025rethinking} show that the negative
log-likelihood of the greedy output is a theoretically grounded
uncertainty measure under the zero-one rule, matching multi-sample
methods without sampling. A persistent weakness is length bias:
even nominally length-normalized measures show statistically
significant linear trends with output length
\citep{vashurin2025line,santilli2025lengthbias}. Probing-based
methods inspect hidden representations where truth and error signals
may appear before they surface in output logits, decoupling
uncertainty from decoder calibration.

Consistency-based methods generate multiple samples and measure
semantic agreement. Semantic entropy clusters samples by NLI and
computes entropy over clusters \citep{kuhn2023semantic}; graph-based
variants compute degree, eccentricity, or spectral features over
pairwise similarity matrices \citep{lin2024generating,jiang2024}.
\citet{nguyen2025snne} use nearest-neighbour entropy that holds up
on long outputs. The bottleneck is sampling cost: 5 to 20
generations per query. Recent work cuts this with importance-sampled
or diversity-penalized proposals
\citep{aichberger2025sdlg,park2025diversity} and beam-search
estimators with variance guarantees \citep{fadeeva2026beams}. A
complementary failure mode is confident incorrectness: when the LLM
is wrong with high agreement, self-consistency mis-rates it as
confident, so \citet{hamidieh2026crossmodel} fold in cross-model
disagreement.

\subsection{Mechanistic hallucination detection}

Hidden states encode signal about factuality
\citep{marks2023,azaria2023,liu2024,ji2024} and many probes consume
a single layer-token slice
\citep{kadavath2022,snyder2024,zou2023,li2024}. ACT-ViT
\citep{barshalom2025} extends this by reading the full layer-token
plane as an activation image and pooling it through a Vision
Transformer; this widens the receptive field but commits to one
score per response, which dilutes the signal as responses grow long
(Figure~\ref{fig:perf_by_length}) and forces one forward per token
when used streaming (Figure~\ref{fig:inference_time}). Fact-probe
\citep{snyder2024,he2024} pushes detection to claim-level granularity
but at the cost of one forward per claim. A second axis of work
probes attention for hallucination patterns
\citep{li2025uqac,duan2024shifting,he2024}; the cross-axis signal it
captures is complementary to the layer-token plane that we operate
on. A third line of work compares hidden representations across
contexts to expose epistemic uncertainty as feature gaps
\citep{bakman2025featuregaps}.
Figure~\ref{fig:signal_propagation} shows that the relevant
activation signal extends across tokens on both sides of a
hallucinated span, the structural fact that motivates our windowed
formulation.

\subsection{Uncertainty in long-form generation}

Long responses interleave correct and uncertain content, which
single-score detectors cannot localize. Decomposition is the standard
remedy: at the claim level via atomic-fact extraction
\citep{min2023,wei2024,zhao2024} or conformal claim-level prediction
\citep{mohri2024}; at the sentence level by re-applying
self-consistency per sentence \citep{manakul2023,band2024}; and at
the token level via per-token probes \citep{he2024,su2024}. The
trade-off is real: claim and sentence pipelines depend on an external
extractor and one model call per unit, while token-level methods are
the only ones compatible with real-time generation, though noisier
when read in isolation. We make the token level usable in practice by
sharing computation across a window of tokens rather than scoring each
in isolation, and by training under weakly supervised temporal
localization.
