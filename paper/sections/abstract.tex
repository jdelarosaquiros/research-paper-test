CoCoA Light approximates sampling-based consistency scores for large language model
(LLM) uncertainty quantification by training a small MLP on middle-layer embeddings,
avoiding the cost of repeated generation at inference time.
However, the original formulation extracts features from a single transformer layer
and trains with mean squared error, which is sensitive to noisy consistency targets
and does not directly optimize for ranking quality.
We propose \method{}, two complementary modifications: (i)~\emph{multi-layer
embeddings}, which concatenate representations from three adjacent layers to
capture features at multiple levels of abstraction, and (ii)~a \emph{Huber loss}
that down-weights outlier consistency targets.
Evaluated on six benchmarks spanning question answering, summarization, and
translation with Mistral-7B under greedy decoding, \method{} improves 22 of 24
PRR$_{0.5}$ metrics over the CoCoA Light baseline, with gains of up to 28.1\%
(XSUM) and 13.0\% (MMLU) on the raw consistency estimator, at no additional
inference cost.
